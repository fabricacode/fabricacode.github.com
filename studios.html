We work thematically and collaborate transdisciplinarily.

Our work is created in studio areas which individually investigate projects along themes. Studio areas are dynamic and take advantage of quick bursts of project development. Each studio investigates a topic for a limited amount of time and then moves on. Since themes are continually changing our researchers continually change as well to accommodate the new skills required for project investigation. Teams shift fluidly and grow and shrink according to the current initiatives. Each studio is run by a studio lead who acts as the principal investigator of that subject matter.

Being and Dying

Ivor Williams

Researching and investigating current approaches to dying, through experimental design focusing on end-of-life care, assisted dying and suicide. Including broader issues around health and the environment, preventative and holistic healthcare, to challenge the notion of death in stark opposition to life. Utilising a wide range of approaches and perspectives, projects will develop new dialogues and models to engage with some of the most deep-rooted taboos in contemporary society.

Using Fabrica as a test-bed and experimental environment. Producing books, essays, films, apps, self-directed research projects – in collaboration with partners – that range from experimental aesthetic reflections – design fictions, media, exhibitions, installations – through to service design- and experience design-led engagements with clients, such as health services, clinics, schools. Follow our activities on tumblr and twitter.

We are looking for:
Interaction designers, writers, coders, film makers and graphic designers.

Document

Ries Straver

The Document Studio focuses on documentary and documentation as an integral part of the creative process. We employ the languages and techniques of audio-visual documentation and digital storytelling to reflect on current social and cultural issues. We investigate the blurry line between fiction and non-fiction. We document with the aim to understand, but we use documentary to pose questions rather than give simplistic answers.

Networked Politics

Marco Ferrari and Joseph Grima

Our Networked Politics studio explores how our various cultures of decision-making, from formal politics to informal protest, are radically changed in the age of the network. We are looking at the changing nature of protest - Occupy, Arab Spring, Istanbul, Wikileaks and beyond - including the relationship between physical spaces, cities and contemporary digital tools. We're interested in the shifting modes and possibilities of contemporary decision-making (currencies such as Bitcoin; cultures such as open source and radical transparencies; governance models like liquid democracy, crowd-funding and crowdsourcing; forms of organisation such as communes and autonomous states).

NetPol creates exhibitions, media, apps and artifacts in order to articulate research about how our various cultures of decision-making, from formal politics to informal protest, are being transformed as a result of The Network. We work on design and communications projects with clients from governments to galleries, in order to effect real change, as well as producing our own media, documentaries, platforms and objects, software, scenarios and events.

We are looking for:
Interaction designers, coders, writers, producers, film makers, architects, urbanists and photographers.

New Journalism

Enrico Bossan

Journalism continues to radically transform, its dynamics — at the mainstream and the edges — reinvented every year throughout the first decades of the 21st century. Yet some patterns also persist. This studio explores the practice, culture and meaning of journalism in our time, through projects. Our particular take on journalism is in being, as the original strapline of Colors had it, “about the rest of the world”, and we understand that “otherness” through culture, geography, time and perspective.

Sound Interaction

Francesco Novara

We explore the relationship between sound and objects, spaces, people and interactions. We use sound as a starting point, but the outcome might be objects, media, events and artefacts. Sound is one of the least understood yet most powerful senses (you can easily close your eyes but not easily close your ears) and we develop projects that articulate the relationship between sound and communication.

Urban Codes

Aaron Siegel

Investigating the dense spaces where people collect and live out their lives. A focus on how people interact with each other and their environment, the norms and standards of living in an urban setting, navigation and communication in urban environments, and the types of artifacts people leave behind both physically and virtually.

Subjects of research include sensing environmental characteristics, visualizing data collected and produced by people and things in urban areas, transportation and communication networks, graffiti and street art culture, activating public space for social engagement and play, and homelessness and nomadic living.

We are looking for:
Creative coders, media artists, architects, web developers, electrical engineers, hardware hackers, mobile developers, graphic designers, urbanists and computer scientists.
